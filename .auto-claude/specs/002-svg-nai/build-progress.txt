Final Root Cause:

PRIMARY (95% confidence):
Missing `primaryContainer` definitions in 15 out of 16 theme presets cause Flutter's
Material 3 tonal calculation to produce colors with insufficient contrast to `primary`,
making icon glyphs invisible against their container backgrounds.

Mechanism:
1. Icons.auto_awesome uses `theme.colorScheme.primary` color
2. Icon rendered in container with `theme.colorScheme.primaryContainer` background
3. When primaryContainer not defined, Flutter calculates it from primary
4. Calculated value often too similar to primary (insufficient contrast)
5. Icon glyph invisible, only container visible ("color block" appearance)

SECONDARY (65% confidence):
ThemeComposer does NOT set iconTheme in ThemeData, causing icons without explicit
colors to inherit from ColorScheme.onSurface, which may also blend with backgrounds.
